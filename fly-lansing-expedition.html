📢 Marketing Campaign

Fly Lansing Campaign

A marketing campaign designed to promote air travel through Lansing by combining clear messaging, strong visual hierarchy, and audience-focused design across multiple advertising formats.

📜 Expedition Brief

The Fly Lansing campaign focused on creating a cohesive advertising system that could communicate a clear message across multiple digital advertising formats. The objective was to promote Fly Lansing as a comfortable and safe travel option while maintaining visual consistency regardless of ad size or placement. Throughout the project, I explored how imagery, typography, branding, and composition could adapt to varying dimensions while preserving a unified campaign identity. The expedition challenged me to balance readability, hierarchy, and visual impact within the constraints of real-world advertising formats.

🎯 The Mission

Design a flexible advertising campaign that promotes Fly Lansing through consistent branding, clear messaging, and adaptable layouts capable of performing effectively across multiple digital ad placements.

🧭 Campaign Development

Advertising design requires communicating a message quickly and effectively. Before creating the final advertisements, I explored layout options, typography hierarchy, and image placement to determine how viewers would engage with the campaign across different formats and sizes. Special attention was given to balancing visual impact with readability, ensuring that the campaign remained effective whether viewed as a large-format advertisement or a smaller promotional asset.

🌱 Lessons from the Trail

Audience Attention Is Limited

This project reinforced the importance of designing for brief moments of attention. Advertising often has only a few seconds to communicate its message, which taught me to prioritize clarity, hierarchy, and simplicity over unnecessary visual complexity.

Strong Hierarchy Drives Communication

The campaign highlighted how typography, scale, and composition influence what viewers notice first. By carefully organizing information, I learned how design decisions can guide audiences toward the most important message without overwhelming them.

One Message, Multiple Formats

Perhaps the most valuable lesson was learning how to adapt a campaign across different advertising formats while maintaining consistency. Creating variations of the same concept strengthened my understanding of cohesive visual communication and scalable design systems.
