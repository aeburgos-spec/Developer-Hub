🧭 Web Design

Corralejos Website Design

A responsive website concept designed to strengthen the digital presence of a family-owned restaurant through thoughtful design, intuitive navigation, and an authentic representation of its brand.

📜 Expedition Brief

The Corralejos Website Design project focused on creating a modern and accessible website concept for a family-owned Mexican restaurant. The objective was to improve the restaurant's online presence by making essential information easier to find while better reflecting the warmth, culture, and personality of the Corralejos brand. Throughout the expedition, I explored website structure, responsive layouts, visual hierarchy, and user experience principles to create a design that would serve both new and returning customers. Special attention was given to mobile usability, clear navigation, and showcasing the restaurant's story, menu, and atmosphere in an engaging and approachable way.

🎯 The Mission

Design a responsive website concept that strengthens Corralejos Bar & Grill's digital presence while making key information—including menus, hours, contact details, and the restaurant's story—easy to access across desktop and mobile devices.

🧭 Planning the Experience

Before designing the final website, I created wireframes to explore layout options, content hierarchy, and user navigation. These early sketches helped identify how customers would access menus, contact information, and the restaurant's story across desktop and mobile devices.

🌱 Lessons from the Trail

Existing Brands Can Still Need Direction

This project taught me how to design within an existing visual identity rather than starting from scratch. By using the original Corralejos logo as the foundation, I learned how to translate established brand elements into a website that felt authentic, warm, and connected to the restaurant’s personality.

Responsive Design Shapes the Experience

Designing for both desktop and mobile helped me understand how layout decisions affect usability. The site needed to make key information easy to find across screen sizes, including the menu, hours, contact details, and restaurant story.

Good Web Design Serves Real People

The biggest lesson from this expedition was that a website should support the needs of both the business and its customers. Clear navigation, readable content, and thoughtful visual hierarchy can make a digital experience feel more welcoming and useful.
