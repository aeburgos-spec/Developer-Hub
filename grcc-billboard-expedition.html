🎨 Environmental Design

GRCC Campus Billboard Campaign

A large-format billboard campaign designed to promote Grand Rapids Community College through bold visual communication, strong typography, and attention-grabbing composition.

📜 Expedition Brief

The GRCC Billboard Campaign focused on creating large-format promotional graphics designed to capture attention and communicate a clear message from a distance. Unlike digital design, billboard advertising requires careful consideration of scale, readability, and simplicity to ensure the message remains effective within only a few seconds of viewing. Throughout the expedition, I explored typography, visual hierarchy, image selection, and composition while designing advertisements intended for real-world environments. The project emphasized balancing impactful visuals with concise messaging to create advertisements that were both engaging and easy to understand.

🎯 The Mission

Design a series of large-format billboard concepts that promote Grand Rapids Community College through clear messaging, compelling visuals, and layouts optimized for quick readability at a distance.

🧭 Design Exploration

Designing for billboards required a different approach than traditional print or web design. Every design decision—from typography size to image placement—had to support immediate communication while maintaining visual balance across large-scale layouts. Throughout the process, I explored multiple concepts and layout variations to determine how hierarchy, color, and spacing could guide viewers toward the primary message in only a few seconds.

🌱 Lessons from the Trail

Designing for Distance

This project taught me that environmental graphics require a different mindset than screen-based design. Every element had to remain readable and recognizable from a distance, reinforcing the importance of simplicity, scale, and visual hierarchy.

Every Second Counts

Because billboards are often viewed for only a few moments, I learned how to prioritize essential information and eliminate unnecessary visual distractions. Strong communication depends as much on what is removed as what is included.

Designing for Real Spaces

Creating work intended for physical environments strengthened my understanding of contextual design. Considering how viewers interact with graphics in motion helped me think beyond the screen and design experiences that function effectively in the real world.
